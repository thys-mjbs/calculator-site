Income vs Essentials Readiness Check (Pro)

Enter your baseline monthly income and essential costs, then add reliability, variability, commitments, buffer targets, and estimation uncertainty. Click Calculate to populate “Your results” below.

Monthly income
Typical take-home income for an average month.
Housing
Rent or bond payment. If shared, enter only your portion.
Utilities
Electricity, water, gas, and required baseline connectivity.
Food
Groceries and household consumables. Exclude eating out.
Transport
Commute and minimum operating costs you cannot avoid.
Minimum debt payments
Only required minimums. Exclude extra repayments.
Income reliability
How much of income is dependable (0.50 to 1.00).
Income variability
Typical monthly swing as a percentage (0 to 40).
Non-negotiable commitments
Mandatory commitments not captured above (school fees, support, insurance, etc.).
Target buffer months
Months of essentials you want covered by cash buffer (0 to 12).
Estimation error margin
How far essentials might be off (0 to 25 percent).
Estimation confidence
Confidence in the values (50 to 100). Lower confidence increases safety loading.

Your results

This tool applies a conservative model. Reliability and variability reduce usable income. Commitments increase essentials. Estimation uncertainty adds a safety loading to essentials. The goal is to avoid “paper coverage” that breaks the first time a month is not average.

What you get here

  • Readiness classification: underprepared, borderline, stable
  • Adjusted income and adjusted essentials
  • Coverage ratio and monthly margin after safety loading
  • Minimum correction target to reach stable coverage
  • Cash buffer target based on your selected buffer months

Limitations

  • Does not assess shocks or future commitments.
  • Outputs depend on your inputs. Low confidence increases safety loading by design.
