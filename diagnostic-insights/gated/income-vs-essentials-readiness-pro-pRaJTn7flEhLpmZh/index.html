Income vs Essentials Readiness Check (Pro)

This version adjusts for income reliability, income variability, commitments, target buffer, and estimation uncertainty. Enter values, then see results populated below.

Monthly income
Reliable take-home income for a typical month.
Housing
Rent or bond payment. If shared, enter only your portion.
Utilities
Electricity, water, gas, and required baseline connectivity.
Food
Groceries and household consumables. Exclude eating out.
Transport
Commute and minimum operating costs you cannot avoid.
Minimum debt payments
Only required minimums. Exclude extra repayments.
Income reliability factor
How much of income is truly dependable (0.50 to 1.00).
Income variability
Typical monthly swing as a percentage (0 to 40).
Non-negotiable commitments
Mandatory commitments not captured above (school fees, support, insurance, etc.).
Target buffer months
Months of essentials you want covered by a cash buffer (0 to 12).
Estimation error margin
How far your estimate of essentials might be off (0 to 25 percent).
Estimation confidence
Confidence in inputs (50 to 100). Lower confidence increases safety loading.

Results appear in the “Your results” section below.

Your results

This section updates when you click Calculate. It applies a conservative model: reliability and variability reduce usable income, mandatory commitments increase essentials, and estimation uncertainty adds a safety loading to essentials.

What the numbers mean

  • Adjusted income is income reduced by reliability and variability.
  • Adjusted essentials include commitments and a safety loading based on error margin and confidence.
  • Coverage ratio is adjusted income divided by adjusted essentials.
  • Buffer target estimates the cash amount needed to cover your adjusted essentials for your chosen months.

Limitations

  • This is a baseline structure diagnostic only. It does not model shocks, future commitments, dependents, or interest rate changes.
  • Outputs are only as good as inputs. If confidence is low, the tool intentionally increases safety loading.
