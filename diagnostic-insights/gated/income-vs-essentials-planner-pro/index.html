Income vs Essentials Planner Pro

Full correction engine: targets, levers, and stress scenarios

Enter your baseline monthly income and essentials, then add paid-only calibration inputs (reliability, fixed commitments, target buffer, confidence, drift risk, and feasibility cap). This tool computes conservative coverage, correction targets (income and expense paths), lever impacts, stress scenarios, and a ranked action plan.

Monthly income
Reliable monthly take-home income (no symbols).
Housing
Rent or home repayment plus unavoidable housing costs.
Utilities
Power, water, basic connectivity required for daily living.
Groceries
Food and household essentials you cannot skip.
Transport
Fuel, public transport, unavoidable commuting costs.
Debt minimums
Minimum required payments (not extra repayments).
Income reliability
How consistent your income is. Lower means a more conservative baseline.
Fixed commitments beyond essentials
Non-negotiable monthly commitments not in essentials (maintenance, fees, support, etc.).
Target buffer depth
How many months of baseline outflow you want as a buffer (0 to 12).
Estimation error margin
How inaccurate your numbers might be. Higher increases targets to reduce surprise.
Essentials drift risk
Expected upward drift in essentials (price creep, variability, seasonality).
Max feasible cut per lever
Practical ceiling for reducing any one lever (used in the ranked plan, not the math).

Assumptions and limits

  • All figures are monthly and treated as baseline inputs.
  • Income reliability reduces income to a conservative baseline.
  • Essentials drift risk increases essentials to a conservative baseline.
  • Estimation error margin increases buffer targets to reduce surprise.
  • This does not assess shocks or future commitments beyond the inputs you enter.
